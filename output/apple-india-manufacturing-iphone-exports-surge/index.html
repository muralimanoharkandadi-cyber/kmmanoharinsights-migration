Why India Is Becoming Apple’s Next Global Manufacturing Hub

India’s transformation into a global electronics manufacturing powerhouse has reached a remarkable milestone. Apple’s iPhone exports from India have surged to $10 billion in the first half of fiscal year 2025, marking a 75% increase from $5.71 billion during the same period last year.

What was once considered an ambitious policy vision under the “Make in India” initiative is now materializing into a tangible industrial shift. Apple, the world’s most valuable technology company, is steadily building an extensive manufacturing ecosystem in India as part of its strategy to diversify supply chains away from China.

The scale and speed of this transition are unprecedented. With multiple global suppliers investing billions of rupees, India is rapidly evolving from a secondary manufacturing base to a core production hub in Apple’s global supply chain.

This transformation is not merely about assembling smartphones. It represents a deeper shift involving component manufacturing, supplier ecosystems, logistics infrastructure, and export competitiveness.

The Strategic Shift Away from China

For nearly two decades, China served as the backbone of Apple’s manufacturing operations. The country’s advanced infrastructure, efficient logistics, and vast supplier ecosystem made it the ideal location for assembling millions of iPhones every year.

However, geopolitical tensions, rising labor costs, and trade uncertainties have compelled Apple to rethink its dependence on a single manufacturing geography.

Several factors have accelerated Apple’s pivot toward India:

1. Geopolitical Diversification

Trade tensions between the United States and China have exposed vulnerabilities in global supply chains. Technology companies are increasingly pursuing a “China Plus One” strategy, seeking additional production bases to mitigate risk.

India, with its large workforce and growing industrial capabilities, has emerged as the most viable alternative.

2. Policy Support from the Indian Government

The Indian government has actively encouraged electronics manufacturing through initiatives such as:

  • Production Linked Incentive (PLI) Scheme

  • Electronics System Design and Manufacturing (ESDM) policy

  • Special Economic Zones (SEZs)

  • Export incentives

These policies significantly reduce manufacturing costs and attract foreign investment.

3. Expanding Domestic Market

India is also one of the fastest-growing smartphone markets in the world. Local manufacturing enables Apple to:

  • Reduce import duties

  • Offer competitive pricing

  • Increase market share in India


Apple’s Manufacturing Ecosystem in India

Foxconn: The Backbone of Apple Manufacturing

Foxconn, Apple’s largest contract manufacturer globally, has been a key player in India’s iPhone production story.

The company operates several manufacturing facilities, including:

  • Sriperumbudur plant in Tamil Nadu

  • Devanahalli facility near Bengaluru

The Devanahalli plant in Karnataka is expected to become one of Foxconn’s largest manufacturing complexes globally, reflecting Apple’s long-term commitment to India.

The Karnataka government has supported this project with ₹6,970 crore in incentives under its Electronics System Design and Manufacturing policy.

Foxconn is also significantly expanding its workforce, hiring thousands of workers to support growing production volumes.

Tata Electronics: India’s Rising Manufacturing Champion

A major development in India’s electronics manufacturing landscape is the entry of Tata Electronics.

The Tata Group has rapidly emerged as a key partner in Apple’s supply chain.

The company operates:

  • Hosur's iPhone manufacturing facility in Tamil Nadu

  • Semiconductor and electronics component manufacturing initiatives

The Hosur plant began operations in April 2025, contributing significantly to India’s export growth.

Tata’s involvement represents a broader shift where Indian conglomerates are increasingly participating in global electronics supply chains.

Apple Suppliers Expanding Investments

Beyond the primary assemblers, Apple’s supplier ecosystem in India is expanding rapidly.

This is a critical development because Apple’s supply chain includes hundreds of specialized component manufacturers.

TD Connex Expansion

Singapore-based TD Connex plans to invest ₹200 crore to expand its manufacturing capacity near Oragadam in Tamil Nadu.

The company specializes in micro-precision components, including:

  • CNC machining

  • Plastic injection molding

  • Metal stamping

  • Smartphone housing components

To support expansion, TD Connex plans to acquire 20 acres of land near its existing facility.

Such investments strengthen India’s ability to manufacture not only final products but also high-precision components.

Yuzhan Technology: India’s First Display Module Manufacturing

Another milestone is the establishment of India’s first smartphone display module manufacturing facility.

Yuzhan Technology, a subsidiary of Foxconn, has begun operations in Tamil Nadu.

The project represents an investment of ₹13,180 crore, approved by the Tamil Nadu government in September 2025.

Display modules are one of the most complex and expensive components in smartphones. Manufacturing them locally significantly enhances India’s position in the global electronics value chain.

Yuzhan has already started exporting assembled display modules to Foxconn plants outside India.

This indicates that India is not only assembling phones but also exporting high-value components globally.


Another important addition to Apple’s supplier ecosystem is Aequs, a Karnataka-based manufacturing company.

The firm has begun trial production of MacBook enclosures and Apple Watch mechanical components.

This development follows the Indian government’s approval of Aequs Infra’s electronics Special Economic Zone in the Hubballi Durable Goods Cluster.

Aequs has extensive experience in precision engineering and aerospace manufacturing, making it well-suited to produce complex components for Apple devices.

The company’s entry into Apple’s supply chain is significant because it reflects India’s increasing capability in high-precision manufacturing.

India’s iPhone Manufacturing Output

The rapid expansion of Apple’s supply chain has dramatically increased production volumes.

In fiscal year 2025:

  • Apple vendors produced $22 billion worth of iPhones in India

  • Nearly 80% of production was exported

  • Total exports reached approximately $17.5 billion

These figures demonstrate how quickly India has moved from a minor assembly hub to a major export base for Apple devices.

Just a few years ago, most iPhones sold globally were assembled in China. Today, India is playing a growing role in meeting global demand.

A Historic First: All iPhone 17 Models Assembled in India

One of the most significant developments is Apple’s decision to assemble all iPhone 17 models in India for global markets from day one.

This includes:

  • iPhone 17

  • iPhone 17 Pro

  • iPhone 17 Pro Max

  • iPhone 17 Air

Previously, premium models such as the Pro and Pro Max were manufactured exclusively in China for several months before production expanded to other countries.

The new approach signals that India is now capable of supporting high-end smartphone manufacturing at launch scale.

This milestone represents a major vote of confidence in India’s manufacturing capabilities.



Economic Impact on India

Apple’s expansion is generating significant economic benefits for India.

1. Job Creation

Electronics manufacturing facilities employ thousands of workers across assembly lines, engineering teams, and logistics operations.

Foxconn alone employs tens of thousands of workers in Tamil Nadu.

2. Export Growth

Smartphones have become one of India’s largest export categories, contributing significantly to foreign exchange earnings.

3. Technology Transfer

Participation in global supply chains enables Indian firms to gain access to:

  • Advanced manufacturing technologies

  • Precision engineering practices

  • Global quality standards

4. Supplier Ecosystem Development

Apple’s presence attracts dozens of ancillary suppliers, strengthening India’s industrial ecosystem.

Challenges Ahead

Despite the impressive growth, several challenges remain.

Infrastructure Bottlenecks

Efficient logistics networks, ports, and transportation infrastructure are essential for electronics exports.

Skilled Workforce Development

High-precision manufacturing requires continuous skill development and training.

Global Trade Risks

Potential trade restrictions or tariffs—particularly from the United States—could affect export competitiveness.

Supply Chain Depth

While assembly operations have expanded rapidly, India must continue developing domestic component manufacturing capabilities.

India’s Future as a Global Electronics Manufacturing Hub

The trajectory is clear: India is steadily becoming one of the world’s most important electronics manufacturing destinations.

Several factors support this outlook:

  • Strong government incentives

  • Competitive labor costs

  • Growing domestic demand

  • Rapidly expanding supplier ecosystems

Industry analysts expect Apple’s India production and exports to surpass previous records by March 2026.

If this momentum continues, India could soon rival China and Vietnam as a major global hub for smartphone manufacturing.

Conclusion

Apple’s manufacturing expansion in India marks a historic shift in the global technology supply chain.

The 75% surge in iPhone exports, billions in supplier investments, and the assembly of all iPhone 17 models in India demonstrate how rapidly the country is emerging as a key production center.

For India, this transformation represents more than industrial growth. It symbolizes the country’s growing integration into high-value global manufacturing networks.

If sustained, this momentum could position India as one of the most influential players in the global electronics industry over the coming decade.
