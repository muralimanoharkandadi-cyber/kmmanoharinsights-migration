Mission Drishti: India’s Private Space Breakthrough in Earth Observation


India’s rapidly growing private space sector is preparing for a major milestone with Mission Drishti, an advanced Earth Observation satellite developed by the Bengaluru-based space-tech startup GalaxEye.

Scheduled for launch in early 2026, likely aboard a SpaceX rocket, Mission Drishti represents a significant technological leap for India’s commercial space ecosystem. Weighing approximately 160 kilograms, it will become India’s largest privately built satellite and will carry one of the highest-resolution indigenously developed imaging sensors.

But what truly sets Mission Drishti apart is its unique fusion of radar and optical imaging technologies on a single satellite platform, a capability that has never before been operationalized globally.

A Global First: Fusion of SAR and Optical Imaging

Mission Drishti integrates two powerful imaging technologies:

Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR)

SAR sensors enable satellites to capture images through clouds, rain, smoke, and darkness. This means reliable data collection regardless of weather or time of day.

High-Resolution Optical Sensors

Optical sensors provide images similar to high-quality photographs, making them easier to interpret for human analysts.

SyncFusion Imaging System

GalaxEye’s proprietary SyncFusion Imaging System combines these two data streams in real time onboard the satellite. The fused dataset, called FusedoSAR, produces richer and more actionable insights than either system alone.

This fusion enables consistent, uninterrupted Earth observation, solving a long-standing challenge in satellite imaging where cloud cover or nightfall often disrupts optical data collection.

Technical Specifications of Mission Drishti

Mission Drishti is designed as a multi-sensor Earth Observation platform optimized for both accuracy and speed.

Key specifications include:

  • Satellite Weight: ~160 kg

  • Spatial Resolution: ~1.5 meters per pixel

  • Sensor Suite: Synthetic Aperture Radar + Multispectral Optical Sensor

  • Fusion Technology: SyncFusion Opto-SAR imaging

  • Data Processing: Onboard processing under 10 minutes

  • Output Dataset: FusedoSAR multi-modal imagery

A 1.5-meter resolution means each pixel represents a 1.5 × 1.5 meter area on Earth, allowing analysts to detect vehicles, infrastructure changes, crop patterns, and terrain details with impressive clarity.

Applications Across Multiple Sectors


The real power of Mission Drishti lies in its versatile real-world applications.

1. Disaster Management

The satellite can provide near-real-time monitoring of floods, earthquakes, landslides, and cyclones, enabling authorities to respond faster and allocate relief resources efficiently.

2. Border Surveillance and Defense

With all-weather imaging capability, Mission Drishti can assist defense agencies in border monitoring, tracking unauthorized movement, and strategic reconnaissance.

3. Agriculture Intelligence

Farmers and agri-tech platforms can use satellite data for:

  • Crop health monitoring

  • Yield prediction

  • Pest and disease detection

  • Soil moisture analysis

4. Infrastructure Monitoring

The satellite can track structural changes in roads, bridges, pipelines, and urban infrastructure, helping governments and insurers assess risks early.

5. Environmental Monitoring

From forest cover tracking to water resource monitoring, the fused imagery will support environmental protection and sustainable planning.

6. Business and Industry Applications

The AI-ready fused dataset enables companies to build applications in:

  • Logistics optimization

  • Insurance risk assessment

  • Smart city planning

  • Resource management

Bengaluru’s Growing Role in Global Space Innovation

Mission Drishti is another example of Bengaluru emerging as India’s space technology hub.

The team behind GalaxEye includes engineers from IIT Madras, reflecting the strong collaboration between India’s premier academic institutions and private deep-tech startups.

India’s space sector has opened significantly to private players in recent years, and companies like GalaxEye are pushing the boundaries of innovation.

The Future: A Global Earth Observation Constellation

Mission Drishti is only the first step in GalaxEye’s ambitious roadmap.

The company plans to deploy a constellation of 8–10 satellites by 2029, enabling near real-time monitoring of the entire planet.

Such a constellation could dramatically enhance global capabilities in:

  • Climate monitoring

  • Disaster prediction

  • Geospatial intelligence

  • National security

  • Resource management

Conclusion

Mission Drishti represents more than just a satellite launch—it symbolizes India’s rising influence in the global space technology landscape.

By pioneering real-time fusion of SAR and optical imaging, GalaxEye is setting a new benchmark for Earth observation capabilities worldwide.

As private innovation accelerates in India’s space sector, projects like Mission Drishti demonstrate how deep-tech startups can transform geospatial intelligence and deliver powerful tools for governments, businesses, and communities alike.

What is the Mission Drishti satellite?

Mission Drishti is a multi-sensor Earth observation satellite developed by Bengaluru-based startup GalaxEye that combines SAR and optical sensors for all-weather imaging.

What makes Mission Drishti unique?

It is the world’s first satellite to fuse Synthetic Aperture Radar and optical imagery in real time onboard, producing a dataset called FusedoSAR.

What is the resolution of Mission Drishti satellite images?

Mission Drishti delivers imagery with a spatial resolution of about 1.5 meters, enabling detailed surface analysis.

When will Mission Drishti launch?

The satellite is expected to launch in early 2026, likely on a SpaceX rocket.

Who developed Mission Drishti?

Mission Drishti is developed by GalaxEye, a Bengaluru-based space-technology startup founded by IIT Madras engineers.
