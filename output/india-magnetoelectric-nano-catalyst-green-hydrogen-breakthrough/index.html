Introduction - 

India’s pursuit of clean energy has entered a transformative phase with a breakthrough 


That sits at the intersection of nanotechnology, materials science, and energy engineering. Researchers at the Institute of Nano Science and Technology (INST), Mohali, have developed a magnetoelectric nano-catalyst based on quasi-two-dimensional α-tellurium nanosheets.

This innovation is not merely an incremental improvement—it represents a paradigm shift in how catalytic processes can be controlled and optimized. At its core lies a powerful idea: what if chemical reactions could be tuned and enhanced using magnetic fields?

Such a capability could dramatically improve the efficiency of green hydrogen production, a cornerstone of global decarbonization strategies.

4) Understanding Magnetoelectric Nano-Catalysts

Catalysts are substances that accelerate chemical reactions without being consumed. Traditionally, catalytic performance depends on surface area, electronic structure, and reaction environment.

A magnetoelectric nano-catalyst, however, introduces a new dimension:

The ability to modulate catalytic activity through external magnetic fields.

This transforms catalysis from a static process into a dynamic and controllable system.


When tellurium is engineered into ultra-thin, quasi-2D nanosheets, it exhibits properties not seen in its bulk form:

  • Emergence of surface electronic states

  • Presence of unpaired electrons

  • Induction of ferromagnetic behavior

These characteristics are crucial for enhancing catalytic efficiency and enabling magnetoelectric coupling.

6) Magnetoelectric Coupling: A New Control Mechanism

Magnetoelectric coupling refers to the interaction between magnetic and electric properties within a material.

In this nano-catalyst:

  • Magnetic fields influence electron distribution

  • Electron flow affects reaction kinetics

  • Reaction pathways can be optimized in real time

This allows unprecedented control over reaction speed and efficiency.


Green hydrogen is produced through water electrolysis powered by renewable energy. However, the process faces challenges:

  • High energy consumption

  • Slow reaction kinetics

  • Cost inefficiencies

The magnetoelectric nano-catalyst addresses these by:

  • Lowering activation energy

  • Enhancing charge transfer

  • Increasing reaction rates

The result: more hydrogen with less energy input.

8) Advantages Over Conventional Catalysts
